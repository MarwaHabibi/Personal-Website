Marwa Habibi is an 18-year-old Afghan girl born in 2007 in the city of Mazar-e-Sharif, Balkh province, into a middle-class family.

Marwa aspires to become Afghanistan’s representative at the United Nations and to establish herself as a renowned writer.

Through her continuous efforts and activism, she is determined to make a positive impact on society and the world.

From a young age, Marwa developed a deep love for books and was passionate about studying and conducting research. Despite facing financial and family challenges, Marwa’s strong enthusiasm for reading and learning never wavered, and each year, she advanced with greater passion and talent.

I pursued my education at Lisah Sheikh Mohammad Sadiq Shahab School until the 8th grade. Unfortunately, due to the Taliban takeover in Afghanistan, I was unable to continue my formal schooling. Despite these challenges, I have continued to pursue learning through online courses and self-directed study.
